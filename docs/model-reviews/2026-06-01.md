## 5. Observation flagged for Robert (NOT changed today)

**The headline systemic index saturates at 83.3 for every great-power-war scenario.**
Ukraine 2022, the present world, and Cuba 1962 all read **83.3** on the 0–100 index even
though their P(WWIII) spans 39 → 65 → 80%. Mechanically: `systemic_index =
100·(rung.level + within_band)/6`; all three sit at the **Great-Power War** rung (level 4),
and `within_band` saturates at 1.0 because each theater's `heat` clamps to 1.0 well before
the scenario is maximal. Only confirmed nuclear *use* (rung 5, Systemic) pushes the index
above 83.3.

I consider this **defensible-by-design, not a bug**: the index is explicitly
"escalation-ladder-aligned" (Kahn/CrisisWatch discrete rungs), so reporting "a theater is
at the Great-Power-War rung" identically for three great-power wars is internally
consistent, and the probability gradient is carried by P(WWIII) exactly as the spec intends
("P(WWIII) is its annualized restatement"). I therefore did **not** alter it unilaterally —
re-scaling the public hero metric is Robert's call, not a same-day edit.

That said, it is worth a decision: if the headline is meant to *visibly* separate "one
theater at war" from "two nuclear superpowers eyeball-to-eyeball," the index currently
cannot, because within-band resolution is destroyed by the `heat.min(1.0)` clamp. Options
to consider (none implemented): (a) let the brink/coupling couplers nudge `within_band`
inside the Great-Power-War band so a nuclear brink rides higher than a single regional war;
(b) soft-compress `heat` (e.g. `tanh`) instead of a hard clamp so the top band retains
resolution; (c) accept the plateau and rely on P(WWIII) for the gradient (status quo).
